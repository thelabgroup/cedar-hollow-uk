Slow days, in two very different landscapes

A stay at Cedar Hollow is mostly about doing less. But when you do want to venture out, our two locations pull in completely different directions — ancient Oxfordshire woodland on one side, the Dorset coast on the other.

At the retreat

  • Hot tub under the canopy

    Several of our retreats have a private hot tub on the deck — best used late, with the lights off and the sky doing the work.

  • Cooking outdoors

    Gas BBQs on the balcony, fire pits, and a pizza oven at Bear Lodge. Bring something from a local farm shop and make an evening of it.

  • Wood-burners and long evenings

    Wood-fired warmth, deep sofas and no particular reason to be anywhere. This is what most guests remember.

  • Stargazing

    Away from town light, the sky over the woodland is genuinely dark. Wrap up and look up.

Oxfordshire — woodland and the city

Our Oxford retreats sit in secluded woodland, with walking straight from the door. When you want a change of pace, Oxford itself is close by — colleges, museums, covered market and a good dinner — and back to the quiet the same evening.

Worth doing

  • Early-morning forest walks before anyone else is up.
  • A day in Oxford, then back to the woods for the hot tub.
  • Village pubs and farm shops in the surrounding countryside.

Dorset — the Jurassic Coast

Our Dorset retreats are a short drive from the beaches and cliffs of the Jurassic Coast, with riverbank walks nearer to hand. Beaver's Den sits low by the water; Bear Lodge is built for a bigger group and a longer weekend.

Worth doing

  • Coast path walking, then fish and chips on the way back.
  • Fossil hunting along the shoreline after winter storms.
  • Riverbank wandering straight from the deck.
